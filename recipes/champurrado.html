Champurrado

This is our take on a traditional Mexican drink that is very popular during Day of the Dead (around Halloween) and at Las Posadas (during the Holiday season).

Author: Diana, our Spanish Program Director

Ingredients

  • 1 cup water
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 1 cup unsweetened soy milk
  • 4 teaspoons unsweetened cocoa or cacao powder
  • 4 teaspoons homemade Date Syrup
  • Â½ cup rolled oats

Instructions

  1. In a pot, bring the water to a boil and cook the cinnamon on high heat.
  2. Add the oats and lower to medium heat. Cook until the oats start to soften.
  3. In a small bowl, mix the soymilk with the cocoa powder and Date Syrup. Add the mix to the pot with the oats and stir together.
  4. Cook for about 3 minutes. Add more soymilk as needed to reach desired consistency of the champurrado. Serve hot. Makes enough for 2 people.
